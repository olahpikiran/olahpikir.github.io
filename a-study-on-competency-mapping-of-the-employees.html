9. The benefit of mind mapping


Kelas: SMA
Pelajaran: English
Kata kunci: mind mapping
Pembahasan:

Mind mapping is useful for saying your opinions in order, from the background information, the core of it and conclusion. Moreover, it's very beneficial too in writing, especially essays and articles. This would help you to organise your ideas in a good outline so that the ideas in writing would be cohesive and well-planned. In addition, mind mapping also gives you an advantage in analysing the whole situation if you're already getting used to with it by heart before you decide something. That way you can be wiser when it comes to critical situation.

30. The topic of meeting 2 is pronouns. Based on the objectives of the study, would you like to explain the functions of pronouns and types of pronouns?


Pronouns are words that are used to replace or refer to nouns or noun phrases. They serve several important functions in a sentence, including:

Substitution: Pronouns are used to replace nouns or noun phrases, which can make sentences shorter and easier to read. For example, instead of saying "Mary went to the store, and Mary bought some bread," we can say "Mary went to the store and she bought some bread," using the pronoun "she" to replace "Mary."

Reference: Pronouns are used to refer to nouns or noun phrases that have already been mentioned. For example, in the sentence "The dog chased the cat, but it couldn't catch it," the pronoun "it" refers back to the noun "cat."

Agreement: Pronouns must agree in gender, number, and person with the nouns or noun phrases they replace or refer to. For example, the pronoun "she" must agree with a female noun, and the pronoun "they" must agree with a plural noun.

There are several types of pronouns, including:

Personal pronouns: Personal pronouns refer to specific people or things and can be either singular or plural. Examples include "I," "you," "he," "she," "it," "we," and "they."

Possessive pronouns: Possessive pronouns show ownership or possession and can be used to replace nouns or noun phrases. Examples include "mine," "yours," "his," "hers," "its," "ours," and "theirs."

Reflexive pronouns: Reflexive pronouns are used to refer back to the subject of a sentence and are always preceded by a personal pronoun or noun. Examples include "myself," "yourself," "himself," "herself," "itself," "ourselves," and "themselves."

Demonstrative pronouns: Demonstrative pronouns are used to point to specific people or things and can be either singular or plural. Examples include "this," "that," "these," and "those."

Interrogative pronouns: Interrogative pronouns are used to ask questions and can be either singular or plural. Examples include "who," "whom," "whose," "what," and "which."

Relative pronouns: Relative pronouns are used to join clauses together and can refer to people or things. Examples include "who," "whom," "whose," "that," and "which."
