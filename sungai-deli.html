3. Jika yang mencemari sungai Deli adalah asam kuat H₂X dengan pH 4 sebanyak 10 liter. Berapa volume basa kuat BOH dengan pH 11 yang dibutuhkan agar air Sungai Deli tidak tercemar lagi?​


Jawaban:

Penjelasan:

asam kuat H₂X dengan pH 4 sebanyak 10 liter

pH = - log H+
4 = - log H+
H+ = 10^-4 M
H2X -> 2H+ + X2-
Kadar H2X = 1/2 x 10^-4 M = 5 x 10^-5 M

basa kuat BOH dengan pH 11
pH + pOH = 14
11 + pOH = 14
pOH = 3

pOH = - log OH-
3 = - log OH-
OH- = 10^-3 M
BOH -> B+ + OH-

Kadar BOH = 1/1 x 10^-3 M = 10^-3 M

Jumlah volume basa kuat BOH :
a. M1.V1 = a. M2.V2
2. 5 x 10^-5 M. 10 L = 1. 10^-3 M. V2
10^-3 = 10^-3. V2
V2 = 1 L

Alur: Deskripsi kronologis tentang hujan deras yang terjadi dari pagi hingga malam hari, dan akibatnya menyebabkan banjir di beberapa kecamatan di kota Medan.

Latar: Kota Medan, sungai babura, sungai denai, sungai deli.

Amanat: Perlunya tindakan pencegahan dan mitigasi bencana banjir di kota Medan, serta pentingnya peningkatan kesiapsiagaan masyarakat dalam menghadapi bencana alam.

1. Medan Tanah Deli

In ancient times the city of Medan was known as Tanah Deli and the condition of the land was swamped around 4000 hectares. Some rivers cross the city of Medan and all boil down to the Malacca Strait. The rivers are Sei Deli, Sei Babura, Sei Sikambing, Sei Denai, Sei Putih, Sei Badra, Sei Belawan and Sei Sulang Saling / Sei Kera.
In the beginning, opening the Medan village was the Patimpus Teacher, located in Tanah Deli, so since the colonial era people always connected Medan with Deli (Medan-Deli). After the era of independence over time the term Medan Deli gradually disappeared so that it became less popular.

In the past people named Tanah Deli starting from Sungai Ular (Deli Serdang) to the Wampu River in Langkat while the Deli Sultanate in power at that time did not cover the area between the two rivers.
Overall the type of soil in the Deli region consists of clay, sand soil, mixed soil, black soil, brown soil and red soil. This is a study from Van Hissink in 1900 which was continued by Vriens' research in 1910 that besides the type of soil like that there was another type of specific clay found. This clay at the time of Dutch colonialism at a place called Bakaran Batu (now South East Medan or Menteng) people burned high quality bricks and one of the brick factories in that era was Deli Klei.

Regarding rainfall in Tanah Deli are classified into two types, namely: Main Maksima and Additional Maksima. Maksima Utama occurs in the months of October to December while Maksima is Supplement between January and September. In detail, rainfall in Medan averages 2000 per year with an average intensity of 4.4 mm / hour.
According to Volker in 1860 Medan was still a jungle and here and there especially in the mouths of the river interspersed with residential settlements originating from Karo and the Malay peninsula. In 1863 the Dutch began to open Tobacco gardens in the Deli which had become the belle of Tanah Deli. Since then the economy has continued to develop so that Medan becomes the city center of government and economy in North Sumatra
