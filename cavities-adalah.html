Pembahasan

Cause and effect, biasanya juga disebut sebagai kalimat sebab-akibat, merupakan kalimat kausalitas yang dibangun dengan melibatkan tindakan yang menjadi alasan terjadinya sesuatu. Kalimat cause and effect dapat diidentifikasi melalui penggunaan kata hubung (konjungsi) yang menandakan sebab dan akibat.

Secara umum, ada empat konjungsi yang umum digunakan dalam kalimat sebab akibat. Keempat konjungsi tersebut adalah because, as, since, dan so.

Jawaban: alveolus

Penjelasan: The human respiratory system starts working when you inhale oxygen through your nose or mouth. Oxygen travels to the back of the throat, then enters the trachea and into the bronchi.

After passing through the bronchi, oxygen will spread into the tubes called bronchioles, then enter the part at the ends called the alveoli. The human body alone has hundreds of millions of alveoli.

Around the alveolus, there are webs of tiny blood vessels called capillary blood vessels. Here, oxygen is absorbed into the blood and begins to travel towards the heart. After that, the heart pumps it so that it can reach all parts of the body.

As cells use oxygen to carry out their respective functions, carbon dioxide begins to form. The gas will enter the blood into the lungs to be expelled by exhalation.

27. tell me everything, you know about the cavities the human speech all types of phonetic active passive articulator


Jawaban:

1) Voice

In English we have both voiced and voiceless sounds. A sound fits into one of these categories according to how the vocal folds behave when a speech sound is produced.

Voiced: Voiced sounds are sounds that involve vocal fold vibrations when they are produced. Examples of voiced sounds are /b,d,v,m/.

If you place two fingers on either side of the front of your neck, just below your jawbone, and produce a sound, you should be able to feel a vibrating sensation. This tells you that a sound is voiced.

Voiceless: Voiceless sounds are sounds that are produced with no vocal fold vibration. Examples of voiceless sounds in English are /s,t,p,f/.

2) Place

The vocal tract is made up of different sections, which play a pivotal role in the production of speech. These sections are called articulators and are what make speech sounds possible. They can be divided into two types.

The active articulator is the articulator that moves towards another articulator in the production of a speech sound. This articulator moves towards another articulator to form a closure of some type in the vocal tract (i.e open approximation, close, etc – define)

The passive articulator is the articulator that remains stationary in the production of a speech sound. Often, this is the destination that the active articulator moves towards (i.e the hard palate).
